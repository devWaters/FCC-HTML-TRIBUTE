Kentaro Miura's Legacy

Kentaro Miura, author of acclaimed hit manga Berserk, had been working in the industry for close to 50 years. 40 of those years working working on Berserk, a staple of the industry. Below is a collection of accolades and accomplishments, and itinerary of his life.

  • 1976 - At 10 years old Miura-san authors his first manga Miuranger that he published for his classmates in a school publication, spanning 40 volumes.
  • 1977 - Miura-san's second manga, Ken-e-no-Michi, was released. It was the first time he had used India ink and helped improve his drawing techniques greatly.
  • 1982 - In highschool he enrolled in an artistic curriculum, publishing worked in the school booklets with his friends. There he met fellow mangaka Kouji Mori and they co-authored a sci-fi doujinshi for Weekly Shonen Sunday, but did not make it past the last round of selections.
  • 1984 - Briefly worked as an assistant to the great George Morikawa, Mangaka of Hajime No Ippo. First prototypes of Guts character design started floating aorund Miura-san's mind at this time.
  • 1985 - Starting art college at Nihon University he submitted short project Futatabi for examination and was granted admission. The project later earned him the 34th Newcomer Manga Award from Weekly Shonen Magazine. Within the same year his next work Noa was published in Fresh Magazine but was not as successful.
  • 1988 - While working for mangaka Yoshiyuki Okamura, also know as Buronson on project King of Wolves Miura-san published a prototype of Berserk in Hakusensha's Monthly ComiComi magazine. The 48 page prototype placed second in ComiComi's Manga School Competition
  • 1989 - After prototyping in Hakusensha's Monthly ComiComi magazine Berserk would start full serialization afterward in Hakusensha's Monthly Animal House magazine, later to be known as Young Animal magazine.
  • 1997 - Berserk gets a full 25 episode anime adaption, supervised by Miura-san and still known as some of the best of berserk adaptions. Produced by OLM, Inc. and airing on NTV in Japan.
  • 1999 - Berserk gets a video game adaption on the unpopular, poorly sold, ambitious console, the Sega Dreamcast. Sword of the Berserk: Guts' Rage, also known as Beruseruku Sennen Teikoku No Taka Hen Wasurebana no Shō in Japan, was a hack and slash action game, being one of the first games to use the mechanic known as Quick Time Events. The game had fairly good reviews, and universal love for the soundtrack done by Susumu Hirasawa, the same composer that worked on the anime.
  • 2002 - Miura-san received the Award for Excellence at the sixth Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prizes, a yearly manga prize award named after Osamu Tezuka, the grandfather of manga, founded and sponsored by Asahi Shimbun.
  • 2006 - Around this time Berserk started going on its often, and extended hiatuses. From here on Berserk never stayed consistent in serialization and would alternate between monthly and irregular releases.
  • 2012 - 3 theatrical movies covering The Golden Age arc were released starting this year, the first 2 in 2012 and the last one the year after.
  • 2013 - Miura released short standalone manga, Giganto Maxia, published by Dark Horse.
  • 2016 - A second 24 episode anime series was broadcast for 2 seasons, to middling reception. This series covered the arcs past The Golden Age
  • 2019 - Short manga Duranki started serialization in Young Animal Zero, produced by Miura's standalone studio, Studio Gaga.
  • 2021 - Sadly, Kentaro Miura-san died of an acute aortic dissection, a tear in his aortic valve that cause him to blead to death. Likely due to stress and the toll that nearly 40 years in the manga industry had taken on him.

Kentaro Miura-san's impact on the industry can not be understated, everywhere you look you can find something inspired by berserk. Characters like Artorias from Dark Souls or Caim from the Drakengard series, shows like Fullmetal Alchemist, Demon Slayer, would not exist had berserk not been around. At the time of release for a series to touch on dark subjects was nothing new, Miura-san himself being heavily inspired by the works of Go Nagai-san, specifically Violence Jack, but the raw visceral nature of Berserk isn't tied to the gore and violence, it tugs at the emotion within us and brings forth a disturbing level empathy towards the characters. We are able to see ourselves, in all facets, in these characters, and in that way it pulls us into the world, and feel it's weight.

Following Kentaro Miura-san's death the anime and manga spaces, and quite frankly, the world at large, deeply mourned his passing. Berserk continues to be authored under the studio he created, Studio Gaga. Helmed by Kouji Mori-san, Miura-san's long time friend and fellow mangaka, Berserk and Studio Gaga have continued to preserve Miura-san's legacy, style, and spirit.

Kentaro Miura-san is but another in a long line of people that have suffered in the manga industry, it's harsh deadlines, cutting editors, and absurdly competitive nature witling down person after person.
Let Kentaro Miura-san's sacrifice to be a staunch reminder that art is to be enjoyed, it is to be made for the love of it and the life it brings, not squeezed out from a person, wrung and sivved from their mind until they are to be thrown away.

Rest in peace, Kentaro Miura.
